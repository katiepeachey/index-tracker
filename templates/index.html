Sources & Data Quality
Where the data comes from and what QA is in place
Source Types
Wikipedia
Community-maintained constituent tables. Generally accurate but may lag official rebalancing by days to weeks. No ticker data for most international indices. Used for 23 of 32 indices.
iShares ETF CSV
BlackRock publishes daily holding snapshots for their ETFs. High confidence when the ETF tracks the exact index. Some indices use a proxy ETF (different index, same universe) — see notes. Includes per-company country data.
Nasdaq Screener API
Official Nasdaq API returning all Nasdaq-listed companies. High confidence. Count varies (~3,300–3,500) as companies list and delist. Used for Nasdaq Composite.
50pros.com / Fortune / Forbes
Third-party mirrors and direct API scraping. Fortune franchise API is currently broken; 50pros.com used as fallback (Fortune 500 only — Fortune 1000 incomplete). Forbes API is year-sensitive.
⚠ QA Limitations
  • No automated cross-validation against official index providers (FTSE Russell, S&P Global, STOXX, Nasdaq, etc.)
  • Wikipedia-sourced indices may be days or weeks behind official rebalancing dates
  • iShares ETF holdings ≈ index composition but are not identical — ETF may hold slightly different weights, ADRs, or cash positions
  • Dual-class shares (e.g. Alphabet GOOGL/GOOG, Fox FOXA/FOX) are deduplicated to one entry per company; the A-class / first-listed ticker is kept
  • To validate: compare against the official index factsheet from the relevant index provider
